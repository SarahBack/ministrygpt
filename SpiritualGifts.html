Spiritual Gifts Assessment

Discover how the Holy Spirit has uniquely gifted you for service

Introduction

A spiritual gift is an expression of the Holy Spirit in the life of believers which empowers them to serve the body of Christ, the church.

Scripture references: Romans 12:6-8; 1 Corinthians 12:8-10, 28-30; Ephesians 4:11; and 1 Peter 4:9-11.

Spiritual Gifts Descriptions

Leadership

Leadership aids the body by leading and directing members to accomplish the goals and purposes of the church.

Administration

Administration enables the body to organize according to God-given purposes and long-term goals.

Teaching

Teaching is instructing members in the truths and doctrines of God's Word for the purposes of building up, unifying, and maturing the body.

Knowledge

The gift of knowledge manifests itself in teaching and training in discipleship. It is the ability to learn, know, and explain God's Word.

Wisdom

Wisdom is the gift that discerns the work of the Holy Spirit in the body and applies His teachings to the needs of the body.

Prophecy

The gift of prophecy is proclaiming the Word of God boldly, building up the body and leading to conviction of sin.

Discernment

Discernment aids the body by recognizing the true intentions of those within or related to the body.

Exhortation

Possessors of this gift encourage members to be involved in and enthusiastic about the work of the Lord.

Shepherding

The gift of shepherding is manifested in persons who look out for the spiritual welfare of others.

Faith

Faith trusts God to work beyond the human capabilities of the people. Believers with this gift encourage others to trust in God.

Evangelism

God gifts his church with evangelists to lead others to Christ effectively and enthusiastically.

Apostleship

The church sends apostles from the body to plant churches or be missionaries, carrying out the Great Commission.

Service/Helps

Those with the gift of service/helps recognize practical needs in the body and joyfully give assistance to meeting those needs.

Mercy

Cheerful acts of compassion characterize those with the gift of mercy. They empathize with hurting members.

Giving

Members with the gift of giving give freely and joyfully to the work and mission of the body.

Hospitality

Those with this gift have the ability to make visitors, guests, and strangers feel at ease and welcome.

Assessment Instructions

This is not a test, so there are no wrong answers. The Spiritual Gifts Survey consists of 80 statements. Some items reflect concrete actions; other items are descriptive traits; and still others are statements of belief.

  • Select the one response you feel best characterizes yourself for each item.
  • Do not spend too much time on any one item. Usually your immediate response is best.
  • Please give an answer for each item. Do not skip any items.
  • Do not ask others how they are answering or how they think you should answer.
  • Work at your own pace.

Your response choices are:

  • 5 — Highly characteristic of me/definitely true for me
  • 4 — Most of the time this would describe me/be true for me
  • 3 — Frequently characteristic of me/true for me–about 50 percent of the time
  • 2 — Occasionally characteristic of me/true for me–about 25 percent of the time
  • 1 — Not at all characteristic of me/definitely untrue for me
